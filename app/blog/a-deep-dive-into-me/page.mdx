# Things I’m Into (Outside Code)

I spend a lot of time coding, but a lot of what shapes my taste and personality comes from things completely outside computer science. Music, films, books, travel — they all sneak into how I think and what I enjoy building.

This is just a snapshot of what I’m into right now.

---

## Music

Music is always playing in the background when I’m working.

My taste leans heavily toward classic rock, psychedelic rock, indie, and artists who care more about atmosphere than punchy hooks. I like long intros, strange outros, and songs that feel better the tenth time than the first.

Some artists and bands I keep coming back to:
- Led Zeppelin  
- Pink Floyd  
- The Velvet Underground  
- Khruangbin  
- The Strokes  
- Indie bands with a slightly vintage or laid-back sound

I’m especially drawn to songs that feel *unrushed*. Tracks where nothing dramatic happens for a while, and that’s kind of the point.

---

## Films

I’m not a big fan of movies that constantly explain themselves.

I usually enjoy films that have depth, have a purpose, are moodier, and visually strong — the kind where you remember scenes more than plot points. I love when a movie feels like every frame could be paused and appreciated.

I tend to like:
- atmospheric films
- character-focused stories
- movies that let silence do some of the work
- purpose driven stories
- movies that leave a mark on you, the ones that make you remember them, the ones that make you think

Though this isn't Letterboxd, I'll list some of my favourites:
- Spirited Away
- Up
- Fight Club
- La Haine
- Little Women

---

## Books

My reading habits are… inconsistent, but intentional.

I don’t read to finish books quickly. I’m fine reading a few pages, stopping, and thinking about them for a while. I like books that feel personal, fragmented, or philosophical rather than tightly plotted.

I’m drawn to:
- introspective writing
- books that explore inner thoughts, society and human psychology more than events
- authors who don’t try to give clean answers

Sometimes I reread the same paragraph multiple times and move on with fewer pages than I expected — and that’s a good reading day for me.

Some of my all time favourites:
- The God of Small Things
- Bombay, Meri Jaan (Writings on Mumbai)
- 1984
- The Book of Disquiet
- White Nights
- And almost anything by Ruskin Bond

---

## Travel

When I travel, I care more about *vibes* than itineraries.

I like walking around cities without a plan, noticing cafés, streets, random bookshops, and how places feel at different times of day. I’m less interested in ticking landmarks off a list and more interested in just existing in a place for a bit.

I’m especially drawn to cities with strong character — places that feel lived-in rather than polished.

Mountains highly appeal to me. I like the calmness, serenity and they give me a sense of humility. 

Travelling is something which is and will be my go to escape.

## Why This Page Exists

None of this is separate from who I am as a computer science student.

The same reason I enjoy slow songs or neo-noir films is the reason I like clean code, understandable systems, and ideas that age well. I’m drawn to things that don’t rush you — things that reward sticking around.

---

### A Small Rule I Try to Follow

If something feels interesting *even when it’s not optimized*, it’s probably worth my time.
