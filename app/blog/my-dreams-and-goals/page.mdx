# The Life I’m Trying to Build

I don’t think of my goals as a checklist.  
They’re more like directions I keep adjusting as I grow.

What I want, more than anything, is to live a full life — one where I try things honestly, grow every year, and don’t look back wishing I’d been braver.

This page is my attempt to put that into words.

---

## Trying Things, Without Regret

One of my biggest fears isn’t failure — it’s *never trying*.

I want to:
- travel widely, not just to see places but to live inside them for a while  
- walk unfamiliar streets, eat unfamiliar food, talk to people very different from me  
- say yes more often than no  

I don’t want a life where everything was “reasonable” and safe. I want stories. I want memories that feel earned.

Even if some attempts fail, I’d rather carry scars than questions.

---

## Kindness and Growth (The Two Non-Negotiables)

If I had to reduce everything to two guiding ideas, it would be these:
- **keep growing**
- **be kind while doing it**

Growth, to me, means staying curious, uncomfortable, and open to changing my mind. Kindness means not letting ambition turn me rigid or self-centered.

No matter what I end up doing — engineering, travel, music, coffee — I want to become a better version of myself *without losing softness*.

---

## Cars, Speed, and the Porsche 911

I’ve always been drawn to cars. Not just as machines, but as ideas.

One dream I’m very honest about:
- **owning a Porsche 911 someday**

It’s not about luxury or showing off. What attracts me is what the 911 represents:
- decades of refinement rather than constant reinvention  
- a machine that rewards skill, patience, and understanding  
- something that becomes better the more you learn how to handle it  

That’s how I want to live too.

Not rushing. Not chasing novelty for its own sake.  
Building mastery slowly, respecting fundamentals, and enjoying the process.

Driving, at its best, is presence. You’re forced into the moment. Life feels similar when it’s done right.

---

## Computer Science (A Craft I Take Seriously)

Computer science is one of the main crafts I want to master in my life.

I’m especially drawn to:
- **machine learning and NLP**, because I love the idea of teaching systems to understand messy human language  
- **operating systems**, where abstractions fall away and you’re forced to face reality — memory, scheduling, performance  
- **product-focused SDE roles**, where technical decisions shape how real people experience software  

I don’t want to just “know tools.” I want to understand systems deeply enough to build things that last.

Good software, to me, feels like a well-engineered car: reliable, understandable, and rewarding to use.

---

## Building Things That Matter

I want to work on products that:
- solve real problems  
- respect users’ time and attention  
- are designed with care, not just metrics  

Whether that’s an AI-powered system, a search product, or something smaller, I want to feel proud of *how* it was built — not just that it exists.

---

## Music and the Guitar

Music keeps me human.

I want to:
- keep learning guitar seriously  
- be able to play songs I love with confidence  
- perform once in front of people, even if I’m nervous  

Music reminds me that not everything needs to be optimized or productive. Some things exist purely because they feel good to do.

That balance matters to me.

---

## Running a Marathon (Once, Properly)

One goal that has nothing to do with careers or creativity:
- **run a full marathon at least once**

Not to prove anything to others — but to myself.

Training for something that long forces discipline, patience, and humility. You can’t fake it. You either show up every day or you don’t.

That lesson carries everywhere else in life.

---

## Coffee and Becoming a Barista

Coffee is another small but meaningful dream.

At some point, I want to:
- work as a barista  
- understand the ritual, not just the caffeine  
- Opening my own cafe someday(fingers crossed!)

There’s something grounding about making something simple, physical, and repeatable — and doing it well for others and for myself.

It’s a reminder that craftsmanship exists at every scale.

---

## Showing Up

And last but not the least, showing up. In good times and in bad, For people that matter to me, and for myself.

---

## How All of This Ties Together

At first glance, this might all seem scattered:
- computer science  
- cars  
- music  
- running  
- coffee  
- travel  

But to me, they’re connected by the same values:
- patience  
- craftsmanship  
- growth  
- presence  
- kindness  

I want to build skills slowly, experience the world fully, and keep choosing depth over shortcuts.

---

### The Life I’m Aiming For

Not perfect.  
Not optimized.  
But honest, curious, and full.

If I can look back one day and say *I tried, I grew, and I was kind*, that’ll be enough.
